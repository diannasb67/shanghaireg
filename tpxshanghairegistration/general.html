General Information

Important!

Travel to China will require applying and obtaining a Visa prior to arrival as well as having a valid passport with at least eight months' validity remaining. The lack of either will result in a fine and immediate deportation. Travelers must have a valid visa to exit China and you must leave China before the expiration of the listed duration of stay.

Please download the Visa Requirements and submit the following items as required by U.S. citizens to request a visa for China no later than March 15, 2019. If you are not a U.S. citizen, please contact us directly for requirements.

  • 1. Valid Passport with at least two blank pages and 8 months of validity remaining
  • 2. Photcopy of Passport
  • 3. Completed Visa Application Form for People's Republic of China
  • 4. Passport photograph
  • 5. Proof of Residency for applicants residing in Alaksa, Northern California, Nevada, Oregon, Washington state and those in the DC jurisdiction
  • 6. Job Description for applicants residing in AL, AR, FL, GA, LA, OK, MS, TX and Puerto Rico
  • 7. Supporting Documents for Tourist Travel - Call Corporate Planners Unlimited for Flight Itinerary and Hotel Confirmation
  • 8. Return overnight shipping label
  • 9. Information Page

General Information

Date May 5-9, 2019
Airport Pudong International Airport (PVR) (approx. 45-60 min. drive to the hotel)
Climate Beijing's May is filled with bright reds and vivid greens everywhere. Normally, the weather is very warm with the temperature reaching 78°F and nighttime lows 50°F.
Time Zone Shanghai is GMT/UTC +12h 16 hours ahead of Pacific Standard time.
Language Mandarin is one of the official languages if Shanghai.
Currency Chinese Yuan Renminbi (CNY) is the currency used. While you can use credit and debit cards for your hotel and other major costs, you'll want to have local currency on you for dining out, shopping and activities, especially before venturing into more remote areas of Shanghai. You can order Chinese Yuan currency through your local bank in advance or exchange at Pudong Airport upon arrival for additional fees. .
Entry Requirements For U.S. Citizens a valid passport and Visa is required for travel to Shanghai

What to Bring

When packing for Shanghai, layering is the key for the spring months. The weather is warm and sunny most days, with a chance of rain. During the colder weather in the early mornings and evenings, you can add layers and remove them for when it is warmer during the day. A warm sweater or light weight rain coat will keep you prepared for Shanghai’s weather.

  • Comfortable walking shoes or tennis shoes for Sightseeing
  • Hat, Visor and/or sunglasses
  • Clothing should be lightweight, but bring a lite jacket or sweater
    • Toiletries and Medical Supplies suggestions: Sunscreen, Toiletries (bath gel, shampoo/conditioner, lotion/soap, tooth brush, make-up etc.)
  • Pack all medications you take regularly, in their original containers from the druggist with the prescriptions clearly visible and only the quantity needed for the duration of the trip. Pack in your carry on luggage.
  • First-aid products (adhesive bandages, Tylenol, anti-bacterial wipes, tweezers)
  • Remember to pack liquids and gels in 3-ounce zip bags if packing them in your airline carry-on
  • Camera, digital, film and/or underwater
  • Electronics Chargers (phone, camera etc.) check on line for the voltage and plugs needed
  • A light backpack or cloth bag is great to have for shopping, sightseeing

Dress Code

Dress Code on Property: Smart and conservative holiday travel wear in the day with flat shoes for walking and steps. For evening we are both in the hotel and off site at local restaurants but only the last evening will cocktail attire for awards night be requested.
